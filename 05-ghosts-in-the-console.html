THE GHOSTS IN THE CONSOLE

"When her son is pulled into a sentient, lethal gaming console designed to house a digital afterlife, a desperate mother must team up with a disgraced pro-gamer to beat an unbeatable god. To save him, they must navigate the Roromandare—a deadly rhythm where a grieving tech genius has trapped a million souls to resurrect his lost daughter—forcing our heroes to hack the architecture of a digital Olympus before a 'Game Over' stops the boy’s heartbeat in the real world."

The Treatment

The story begins with a grieving mother who discovers that her son’s "disappearance" isn't physical—it's digital. He has been absorbed into the "Roromandare," a console that promised a way to play with the deceased, but instead acts as a soul-trap. She tracks down a legendary but disgraced pro-gamer living in the shadows of the city. Together, they perform a "forced entry" into the console’s hardware. The world inside is a digital Olympus—a sprawling, gothic architecture built of code and memories. Every level is a psychological trial designed by a genius who couldn't let go of his own daughter. The climax centers on a high-speed "Rhythm Battle" within the console's architecture. They aren't just fighting for a high score; they are fighting to desync the console from the boy's actual heart rate. It’s a race against the "System Refresh" that threatens to overwrite the boy’s consciousness forever. In the end, they must decide if some ghosts are better left in the machine or if the entire architecture of the digital afterlife must be burned to the ground.
