The Treatment

The story opens in the quiet, meticulously organized studio of a forensic artist who spends his nights composing classical symphonies. He sees the world in layers—harmonies, structures, and blueprints. While auditing his old 1990s digital archives, he discovers a startling structural match between his early work and the architecture of the world’s largest digital payment platform. The "Purchase Pilot" wasn't just an idea; it was a sophisticated engine for global commerce that he had mapped out decades ago. As he begins to pull the thread, he realizes his vision was systematically "hollowed out" by a tech titan who is now a household name. He isn't just seeking a payday; he is seeking the restoration of his identity as the true architect of the modern economy. The climax is a visual and intellectual masterpiece. Using his skills as a forensic artist, he creates a "visual symphony"—a complex overlay that proves, node-for-node, that the billionaire's empire is built on the skeletal remains of his original code. It is a battle of the soul versus the machine, exploring the themes of intellectual theft, the invisibility of Black genius in tech history, and the power of seeing the structure beneath the surface.
