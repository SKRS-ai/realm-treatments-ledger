The Treatment

The story centers on an intellectually fearless traveler who refuses to let his consciousness be "indexed" by a society that demands total transparency. In a future where neural privacy is considered a crime, he lives as a ghost in the system, treating his thoughts as private soil. He travels to the edges of the map—both geographically and mentally—searching for a "dead zone" where the government's invasive cognitive laws cannot reach. Along the way, he encounters a underground network of "Sovereigns"—individuals who have mastered the art of mental encryption to keep their inner worlds free from corporate and state interference. The climax is a legal and psychological standoff. When he is finally apprehended, he must defend his right to mental sovereignty in a high-stakes "Consciousness Court." He proves that if a man does not own his thoughts, he owns nothing. The film is a powerful exploration of the final frontier of human rights: the space between our ears.
