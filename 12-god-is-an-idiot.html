The Treatment

The story follows Arthur, a man who has had enough of the "bugs" in reality. From the way knees are designed to give out at fifty to the general unfairness of the weather, Arthur decides that the "Architect" of the universe is, at best, a mediocre engineer. What starts as a series of angry letters to local newspapers evolves into a global "Divine Protest." Arthur’s list of grievances—The Handbook of Better Design—becomes a viral sensation. He demands a meeting with the Management. The climax is a surreal, high-stakes celestial courtroom battle where Arthur gets exactly what he asked for: a chance to pitch his improvements to the Divine itself. However, as he tries to fix the "shoddy craftsmanship," he realizes that every change he makes to "optimize" life has unintended, hilarious, and devastating consequences for humanity. It’s a witty exploration of human hubris, the complexity of creation, and the realization that maybe the world isn't broken—it’s just far more complex than a mortal IT manager can troubleshoot.
