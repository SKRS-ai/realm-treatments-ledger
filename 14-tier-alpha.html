The Treatment

The world of Tier Alpha is a frictionless paradise built on the "Perfect Memory" of a global ledger. Every act of kindness, every productive hour, and every social contribution is tracked and taxed. Those with high "Society Credit" live in Tier Alpha; those without are relegated to the unrecorded slums. Our protagonist is a man whose identity was "zeroed out" by a glitch in the old system. He lives as a ghost, performing deep acts of unrecorded kindness that the system cannot see. When he discovers that the architect of this perfect world is the same man who stole his family's legacy, he begins a "social heist." Instead of breaking in with weapons, he uses a network of people who "vouch" for his character, transferring their own credits to him in a grassroots rebellion of kindness. He ascends through the tiers, eventually reaching the crystalline towers of Tier Alpha. The film concludes with a confrontation not of violence, but of data—proving that a system with a perfect memory is only as just as the person who wrote the initial code.
